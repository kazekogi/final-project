Why I decided to Learn Japanese

I've always been an introvert my whole life. So after graduating from High School, I decided to on a trip to Japan to be more social. I made a ridiculously detailed list about the trip: which hotel to stay in, the places to visit, which night bus to take, etc... However, the virus rolled around the corner so I had to abandoned all my plans. So instead, I decided to start picking up the language in preparation for my visit to Japan.

My Approach to Learning Japanese

After wasting two weeks on Duolingo and not learning anything, I decided to learn on my own. The first thing I learned was the alphabets using this website. I then picked up the "Genki I" book and started learning grammar. This was well for a while until I encountered the third Japanese writing system, Kanji. The book started introducing many Kanji's and I couldn't understand them. After doing some research, I picked up yet another book to help me remember the Kanji's. And so for the past 4 months, I've been going back and forth between those two books, learning new things each day. While my intial goal was to just learn a little Japanese to get by during my trip, I now want to be able to fool every Japaneses I come across to think that I'm a native speaker.
